Project

Project Scope

This project provides several overlay maps to introduce the audience to the Bureau of Indian Affairs (BIA) Southwesten Region and Reservations to gain a perspective on the digital divide created by lack of access to either Fixed Broadband or Mobile Internet for Native Americans.

Maps

A series of two interactive overlay Maps are provided that detail the Reservation areas and BIA Regions overlaying the 2016 Federal Communications Commission (FCC) data on Fixed and Broadband Internet Access for 2016 for the study area. The second interactive Map provides a visual introduction for the audience to the Trail of Tears passage to the Reservation resettelment Lands.

Map 2

The Trail of Tears Map provides a visual image of the actual routes traveled by Native Americans between 1831 and 1877 to the resettlement Reservations. More than 100,000 Native Americans were forcibly relocated under the Indian Removal Act, that was both an act of genocide and ethnic cleansing. The Act was signed into law on May 28, 1830, by United States President Andrew Jackson.
